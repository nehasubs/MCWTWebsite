Seven Days With No Technology



It's like the world has shut down. For one week people all around the world only have access to two types of technology: a mere landline phone and basic electricity. In today's technology driven world, this will serve as a great challenge for some people. With society’s constant obsession with phones, laptops, and televisions, “unplugging” is not as easy as it seems. According to The MIT Technology Review, the average adult spends almost 23.6 hours per weeks online.

On another hand, letting go of our digital reliance may actually help us unwind. Being a busy high schooler, it seems like I barely have time to do my homework, let alone connect with my loved ones. Without technology we may actually sit down and try to connect with our loved ones; interacting with other people would be our only form entertainment. This website will analyze all of these factors by first looking at the initial shock people will have about this situation (Tab 2). Then it will highlight some of the struggles that people will face during these 7 days (Tab 3). Lastly, it will chronicle the reflections people will make about themselves and their lives because of the lack of technology.

I would like to thank the Michigan Council of Women in Technology, for giving me the opportunity to compete for the chance of becoming a finalist. I hope this website not only demonstrates my Web Development skills, but also helps people be less dependent on technology.
